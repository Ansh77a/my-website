How to Start Investing (Even with $50)

Pick Your Accounts

Open a low‑fee brokerage for stocks and ETFs, and a reputable crypto exchange for digital assets. Keep long‑term investments separate from trading funds. If available in your country, consider tax‑advantaged accounts for traditional assets.

Automate Small, Regular Buys

Use dollar‑cost averaging (DCA): buy a fixed amount on a set schedule. This helps reduce the risk of buying at a single bad price and builds discipline.

Diversify Intelligently

Combine broad‑market ETFs with a conservative allocation to BTC and ETH. Keep a cash buffer for emergencies so you’re never forced to sell at the wrong time.

Tip: Write a one‑page plan: goals, contributions, target allocation, and rules for rebalancing. Revisit quarterly.
Crypto

Crypto 101: From Wallets to On‑Chain

Custodial vs. Self‑Custody

Exchanges are convenient, but self‑custody gives you full control. Use a hardware wallet for larger balances and store your seed phrase offline in multiple secure locations.

Stablecoins & Transfers

Stablecoins like USDC can move value between exchanges and chains quickly. Always test with a small amount first and verify the correct network (e.g., Ethereum vs. Layer‑2).

Fees & Networks

Different chains have different fees and speeds. If costs are high on a mainnet, consider reputable Layer‑2 networks for cheaper transactions.

Reminder: Never share your seed phrase. No legitimate support agent will ever ask for it.
DeFi

DeFi Basics: Earning Yield Without Getting Rekt

What Is DeFi?

DeFi lets you lend, borrow, and trade directly on‑chain through smart contracts. Returns can be attractive, but risks include volatility, smart‑contract bugs, and governance changes.

Simple, Lower‑Risk Options

Start with conservative strategies like lending blue‑chip assets on audited protocols. Avoid double‑digit APYs you don’t understand; yields come with trade‑offs.

Understand Impermanent Loss

Providing liquidity to AMMs can lose value versus holding your assets separately. Learn the math before you deposit, and size positions accordingly.

Security

Security Checklist: Protect Your Money

Critical Habits

  • Use a hardware key or app‑based 2FA. Avoid SMS 2FA.
  • Separate email for exchanges and wallets.
  • Sign only what you understand; read transaction prompts.
  • Keep backups of keys offline; test recovery.

Red Flags

  • Promises of guaranteed returns.
  • Unverified token contracts and fake airdrops.
  • “Support” DMs asking for your seed or remote access.
  • Links that ask you to re‑enter your seed phrase.
Tip: Maintain a small, separate “hot” wallet for experiments. Keep your vault wallet offline.
Taxes

Taxes & Tracking: Stay Organized

Track Every Move

Log deposits, swaps, transfers, and gas costs. Good records reduce stress at tax time and help you analyze performance across chains and wallets.

Classify Activity

Separate trading from income (staking rewards, airdrops). Label internal transfers so software doesn’t misread them as taxable events.

Export Friendly

Use wallets and tools that support CSV export or direct API connections. Consistency saves time and prevents errors.
